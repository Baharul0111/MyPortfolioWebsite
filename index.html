Selected Publications

AquaFusionNet: A Robust Underwater Aquatic Defect Detection Using Feature Fusion and Attention‑Based Deep Learning Model

Conference: IEEE Transactions on Image Processing (in preparation)

Authors: Baharul Islam, Nasim Ahmad, Mehbub Alam, Ruhul Amin Hazarika, and Sk Mahmudul Hassan.

Abstract: Underwater infrastructure including pipelines, ship hulls and offshore platforms frequently suffers from biofouling, structural holes and vegetation overgrowth, creating ecological and economic risks. Visual inspection is difficult because poor visibility, variable illumination, and highly textured surfaces limit methods based solely on handcrafted or deep features. We present AquaFusionNet, a hybrid defect classification framework that fuses embeddings from four pretrained backbones (EfficientNet-B0, ResNet-50, SENet-50 and a Vision Transformer) with complementary handcrafted colour, gradient and texture descriptors via a trainable attention module. The attention mechanism lets the network emphasise the most informative cues without discarding fine-scale detail. Evaluated on a corpus of 2228 underwater images spanning three defect classes, with 445 held out for testing, AquaFusionNet achieves 98.43% accuracy, 98.18% precision, 97.79% recall, 96.06% intersection-over-union, and a 97.97% F1-score, outperforming eleven competitive baselines, including ResNet-152 and EfficientNet-B0. Major contributions of this work include a unified attention-based feature-fusion architecture that integrates handcrafted and learned representations, a thorough evaluation against lightweight CNNs and transformers.

Frontiers of Foundation Models: Bridging Domain Gaps with Large Language Models Through Practical Use Cases: A Comprehensive Review

Conference: IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials (in preparation)

Authors: Muhammad Owais, Mehbub Alam, Baharul Islam, Nasim Ahmad, Yazid Musthfa and Irfan Hussain.

Abstract: This paper reviews emerging applications of Large Language Models (LLMs) in less-explored domains, such as precision agriculture and crop management, marine life conservation and oceanography, physics, disaster management and environmental conservation, enhanced virtual reality therapy, business management, forensic science and digital forensics, E-sports, gaming strategy and sports analysis. These applications illustrate the potential of LLMs to enhance operational efficiency, data accessibility, and innovations in various fields. We discuss the challenges to adoption, particularly among traditional faculty, and present MATLAB-based simulation examples to demonstrate practical applications. Future directions and solutions are proposed to support adoption and adaptation of LLMs across diverse fields

NBF at SemEval‑2025 Task 5: Light‑Burst Attention Enhanced System for Multilingual Subject Recommendation

Conference: SemEval 2025 (submitted)

Authors: Baharul Islam, Nasim Ahmad, Ferdous Ahmed Barbhuiya and Kuntal Dey.

Abstract: We present our system submission for SemEval 2025 Task 5, which focuses on cross-lingual subject classification in the English and German academic domains. Our approach leverages bilingual data during training, employing negative sampling and a margin-based retrieval objective. We demonstrate that a dimension-as-token self-attention mechanism designed with significantly reduced internal dimensions can effectively encode sentence embeddings for subject retrieval. In quantitative evaluation, our system achieved an average recall rate of 32.24% in the general quantitative setting (all subjects), 43.16% and 31.53% of the general qualitative evaluation methods with minimal GPU usage, highlighting their competitive performance. Our results demonstrate that our approach is effective in capturing relevant subject information under resource constraints, although there is still room for improvement.

Leveraging LLMs and Deep Learning for Driver Drowsiness Image Categorization

Conference: IEEE GCON 2025 (submitted)

Authors: Nasim Ahmad, Baharul Islam, Mehbub Alam, and Sudipta Majumder.

Abstract: Road accidents caused by driver drowsiness are one of the leading causes resulting in many injuries and deaths every year. In this paper, we propose a new method for driver drowsiness detection, combining LLM and deep learning techniques. Our approach utilizes behavioral information, including facial expression and eye movement, to precisely measure drowsiness. Unlike existing approaches that tend to rely on limited types of data and simple algorithms, our system utilizes the strengths of deep learning for feature extraction coupled with the contextual understanding of LLM. This integration improves accuracy and reliability, whereas previous methods suffered from variability in driving conditions and fatigue indicators. Our model achieved an accuracy of 99.25%, average precision of 99%, average recall of 99% and average F1 score of 99% in our test dataset.

Design and Development of an AI‑Powered Symptom‑Based Ethnomedical Recommendation System

Conference: National Conference on Integrating Ethnobotany & AI for Future Healthcare Solutions (Viksit Bharat @ 2024) ( Submitted)

Authors: Nasim Ahmad, Jia Garodia, Jupitoora Kaushik, Sudipta Majumder, Baharul Islam and Md Noor Ali.

Abstract: The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) into traditional ethnomedical practices presents a promising avenue for enhancing healthcare accessibility and efficacy. This study introduces the design and development of an AI-powered symptom-based ethnomedical recommendation system tailored to the diverse medicinal practices of the Upper Brahmaputra Valley, Tea Garden Areas, and Assamese Plains. Leveraging a dataset comprising 5,000 records across 12 unique symptoms and 10 medicinal plants, we employed a neural network model to predict effective remedies based on user-reported symptoms. The system utilizes natural language processing techniques for symptom tokenization and sequence padding, followed by a classification algorithm trained to map symptoms to appropriate remedies. The model achieved an accuracy of 91.32%. Additionally, the system provides alternative recommendations, enhancing its utility for practitioners and users alike. The proposed system not only bridges the gap between traditional knowledge and modern technology but also provides a scalable framework for integrating ethnomedical practices into contemporary healthcare systems.
